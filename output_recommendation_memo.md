\# Recommendation Memo: Onboarding \& Activation Campaign A/B Test



\## 1. Executive Summary



The objective of this experiment was to evaluate whether the new onboarding and activation campaign improves user conversion and early engagement compared to the existing onboarding experience.



The analysis shows that the Treatment group outperformed the Control group across several key metrics, including onboarding completion, engagement, and paid conversion rate. Statistical testing confirmed that the improvement in paid conversion rate is significant, indicating that the observed lift is unlikely to be due to random chance.



Although the new onboarding experience increased support ticket volume, other guardrail metrics such as refund rate and engagement score did not indicate major business risks. Based on these findings, a phased rollout of the new onboarding experience is recommended.







\## 2. North Star Metric



\*\*North Star Metric:\*\* Paid Conversion Rate



Paid conversion rate was selected as the primary success metric because it directly measures the campaign's impact on subscription revenue. Since the business operates on a subscription-based model, increasing the number of paying customers is the most important indicator of success.

\## 4. Experiment Result Summary



| Metric                     | Control | Treatment | Observation            |

| -------------------------- | ------- | --------- | ---------------------- |

| Trial Start Rate           | 25.11%  | 29.09%    | Improved               |

| Onboarding Completion Rate | 15.58%  | 21.26%    | Improved               |

| Paid Conversion Rate       | 3.17%   | 6.99%     | Significantly Improved |

| Average Revenue per User   | 51.75   | 53.88     | Slight Improvement     |

| Engagement Score           | 57.03   | 62.93     | Improved               |

| Average Days to Convert    | 8.86    | 6.40      | Faster Conversion      |



The Treatment group consistently performed better than the Control group across the majority of business metrics.







\## 5. Hypothesis Test Interpretation



A two-proportion Z-test was conducted to compare the paid conversion rates between the Control and Treatment groups.



\* \*\*Null Hypothesis (H₀):\*\* There is no difference in paid conversion rates between the two groups.

\* \*\*Alternative Hypothesis (H₁):\*\* There is a significant difference in paid conversion rates between the two groups.

\* \*\*Significance Level:\*\* 0.05

\* \*\*P-value:\*\* 0.0017



Since the p-value is less than 0.05, the null hypothesis was rejected. This indicates that the increase in paid conversion rate observed in the Treatment group is statistically significant.







\## 6. Guardrail Analysis



Three key guardrail metrics were evaluated:



\### Refund Rate



The refund rate remained very low in both groups, suggesting that the increase in conversions was not driven by low-quality subscriptions.



\### Support Ticket Rate



The Treatment group showed a higher support ticket rate compared to the Control group. This may indicate that some users experienced friction or confusion during onboarding.



\### Engagement Score



Users exposed to the new onboarding experience demonstrated higher engagement levels, indicating stronger product adoption and user interest.



Overall, the guardrail analysis does not reveal any major risks that would prevent rollout, although support-related issues should be monitored closely.





\## 7. Segment-Level Insights



Segment analysis showed that the Treatment group generally outperformed the Control group across multiple customer segments, including region, device type, and traffic source.



The strongest improvements were observed among users acquired through referral channels and mobile devices, suggesting that these segments respond particularly well to the new onboarding experience.



No major segment-level declines were identified.







\## 8. Final Recommendation



\### Recommendation: Launch the New Onboarding Experience



The experiment results provide strong evidence that the new onboarding campaign improves business outcomes.



Key reasons supporting the launch include:



\* Significant improvement in paid conversion rate.

\* Higher onboarding completion and engagement levels.

\* Faster conversion times.

\* No meaningful increase in refund rates.



A phased rollout is recommended to minimize operational risks and allow additional monitoring.





\## 9. Risks and Limitations



\* The increase in support ticket volume suggests that some onboarding steps may require optimization.

\* The experiment duration may not fully capture long-term customer retention and churn behavior.

\* Results may vary across future customer cohorts and acquisition channels.



These limitations should be considered when interpreting the results.





\## 10. Next Steps



1\. Roll out the new onboarding experience to 50–75% of users initially.

2\. Continue monitoring support ticket volume, refund rates, and customer satisfaction.

3\. Investigate onboarding steps that may be causing user confusion.

4\. Conduct follow-up experiments to optimize the onboarding flow further.

5\. Track long-term retention and churn metrics to validate sustained business impact.
