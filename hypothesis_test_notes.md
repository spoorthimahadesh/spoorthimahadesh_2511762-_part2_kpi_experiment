###### Should the new onboarding and activation campaign be rolled out to all users?

###### To answer this question, the experiment compares the performance of the existing onboarding experience (Control group) with the new onboarding experience (Treatment group).

###### 

###### 1\. Null Hypothesis (H₀)

###### The new onboarding campaign does not significantly impact the paid conversion rate.

###### **H₀:** There is no significant difference in paid conversion rates between the Control and Treatment groups.


H\_0: p\_{control} = p\_{treatment}

---

###### 2\. Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)

###### The new onboarding campaign significantly impacts the paid conversion rate.

###### **H₁:** There is a significant difference in paid conversion rates between the Control and Treatment groups.


H\_1: p\_{control} =! p\_{treatment}

---

###### 3\. Type of Test



###### A **two-tailed test** will be used because the goal is to determine whether the new onboarding experience performs differently from the existing experience, regardless of whether the effect is positive or negative.



###### 4\. Significance Level

###### The analysis uses a significance level of **α = 0.05**.

###### This means that if the p-value is less than 0.05, the result will be considered statistically significant.



###### 5\. Metric Being Tested



###### **Primary Metric:** Paid Conversion Rate

###### Paid Conversion Rate is calculated as:

###### 

###### Paid Conversion Rate = Number of users converted to paid / Total number of users



###### 6\. Reason for Choosing This Metric

###### Paid conversion rate was selected because it directly measures the business objective of increasing subscription revenue. Since the company operates on a subscription-based model, converting users into paying customers is the most important indicator of success. Although metrics such as onboarding completion and engagement provide useful insights into user behavior, paid conversion rate has the strongest connection to business growth and revenue generation.



###### 7\. Interpretation Logic

* ###### If **p-value < 0.05**, reject the null hypothesis and conclude that the new onboarding campaign has a statistically significant impact on paid conversion rate.
* ###### If **p-value ≥ 0.05**, fail to reject the null hypothesis and conclude that there is insufficient evidence to suggest a significant difference between the Control and Treatment groups.
* ###### The final rollout decision should consider both statistical significance and guardrail metrics such as refund rate and support ticket rate.
